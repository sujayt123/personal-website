To facilitate the orientation process, each residential college of Rice University distributes a booklet to incoming students that introduces them to the leaders (so-called "O-Week advisors") of their future communities. Superficially, the publication seems a little facetious. More often than not, it confuses new students by expounding on the inside jokes, out-of-context quotes and zany escapades accumulated by three generations of Rice inhabitants.

And yet, precisely because of this, it is touching in its own way. In writing my bio, for instance, my good friend Daniel Williams managed to capture the essence of my existence rather beautifully. Keep in mind this was written before I began my formal study of computer science.
